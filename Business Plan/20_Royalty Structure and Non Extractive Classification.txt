FILE 20: ROYALTY STRUCTURE AND NON-EXTRACTIVE CLASSIFICATION

PROJECT: XTP-1 AURIC SYNTHESIS

ENTITY: GALALITH BIOPLASTICS INC.

CLASSIFICATION: ADVANCED INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURING

I. NON-EXTRACTIVE EXEMPTION FROM CROWN ROYALTIES

Galalith Bioplastics Inc. operates as an advanced manufacturing entity rather than a traditional mining operation. Because the high-purity gold output is synthesized through the XTP-1 solid-state protocol using industrial feedstock rather than being extracted from Crown mineral land, the corporation is exempt from provincial mineral royalty regimes in both Saskatchewan and Alberta. This classification ensures that the corporation retains one hundred percent of the value of the synthesized output, avoiding the typical one to five percent royalty burden associated with traditional gold mining.

II. INDUSTRIAL REVENUE VS. MINERAL TAXATION

For the purposes of financial reporting and bank audit trails, revenue generated from the XTP-1 protocol is categorized as Industrial Manufacturing Revenue. This distinction is critical for maintaining compliance with the Canada Small Business Financing Program as it separates the corporation from the volatility and specific regulatory levies of the extractive sector. By utilizing 8B and 12B graphite substrates and synthetic quartz resonators as inputs, the process is legally defined as a molecular refinement activity, ensuring a zero percent Crown royalty rate on all annual yields.

III. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ROYALTY GENERATION

While the corporation does not pay extraction royalties, it establishes a secondary revenue stream through the licensing of the XTP-1 protocol. Under the protection of USPTO and UKIPO patent filings, Galalith Bioplastics Inc. can collect technology royalties from third-party industrial partners. These licensing agreements are projected at a rate of five to seven percent of gross sales for authorized use of the magnetic transport technology. This transforms the corporation's intellectual property into a globally portable, royalty-generating asset that is independent of physical production volumes.

IV. NET SMELTER RETURN AND FEEDSTOCK SECURITY

In scenarios where Galalith Bioplastics Inc. processes third-party high-value concentrates or tailings, the corporation utilizes Net Smelter Return agreements. These contracts are standardized at one to two percent depending on the purity of the input material. However, for the primary 4N and 6N synthesis model where the corporation owns the entirety of the hardware and seed material, the internal royalty rate remains at zero. This maximizes the profit spread between the cost of electricity and magnetic array maintenance versus the global spot price for high-purity industrial gold.

V. JURISDICTIONAL STABILITY AND ROYALTY TAX CREDITS

By anchoring operations within the Saskatchewan regulatory framework, the corporation remains eligible for the Saskatchewan Mineral Exploration Tax Credit and other industrial incentives. While the corporation is not an explorer, the technical build-out of the "Auric Apparatus" qualifies for research-based credits that can be applied to offset corporate income tax. This provides a fiscal buffer against any future changes to the Alberta royalty framework following the 2026 referendum, ensuring that the corporation's cost of production remains predictable and protected.
