Technology Readiness Levels (TRL) are a standard measurement system used to assess the maturity level of a particular technology during its development and acquisition phase. Originally developed by NASA in the 1970s, the scale is now used globally by government agencies and industries to manage risk and provide a common language for evaluating technical progress.

The scale ranges from 1 to 9, where TRL 1 represents basic scientific research and TRL 9 indicates a technology that is fully proven through successful operations in its final environment.

The 9 Technology Readiness Levels

TRL 1: Basic Principles Observed – Initial scientific research begins to be translated into applied research and development.

TRL 2: Technology Concept Formulated – Practical applications of the basic principles are identified, though they remain speculative with little to no experimental proof.

TRL 3: Experimental Proof of Concept – Active research and development begins with analytical and laboratory studies to physically validate the technology's feasibility.

TRL 4: Technology Validated in Lab – Basic technological components are integrated to establish that they will work together in a low-fidelity laboratory environment.

TRL 5: Technology Validated in Relevant Environment – The fidelity of the technology increases significantly as it is tested in a simulated or relevant environment.

TRL 6: Technology Demonstrated in Relevant Environment – A fully functional prototype or representational model is verified in a high-fidelity laboratory or simulated operational environment.

TRL 7: System Prototype Demonstration in Operational Environment – The prototype is demonstrated in an actual operational environment, such as a vehicle or in space.

TRL 8: System Complete and Qualified – The actual technology is completed and proven to work in its final form and under expected conditions.

TRL 9: Actual System Proven in Operational Setting – The technology is in its final form and successfully proven through deployment in real-world conditions.
